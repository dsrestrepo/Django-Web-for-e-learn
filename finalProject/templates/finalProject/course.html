statistical inference

What is?

The Statistical Inference is a way of analyzing properties of a population based on a sample
for example we can know the distribution of probabilities to know if a hypotesis is true, or see the behavior of a population based just on a sample of a data

We can see applications in:

  • Investigation in general:
    • Used to find the confidence results of a investigation
  • Data Science:
    • Used to abstract the behavior of all a entire population data form a sample
  • Life sciences:
    • Used to make test for example of a vaccine or to understand the distribution of some characteristics like genome in a population from a sample.

Example:

We are working with a laboratory to find out what effect can have a new product on the weight of the people of this city We have the hypotesis that this new product decrease people's weight, but we can't do a test for all the city But we can use the statistical inference, taking a sample of all the city and with that sample prove our hypothesis.
Null Hypothesis:
This means that the mean doesn't have any change respect to how it soposed to be
\[H_{0}\]
Alternate hypothesis:
That means that our mean has change (less or more) respect to what it was supposed to be without the product, for our example this means that the product will change the mean weight less or more
\[H_{1} \neq H_{0}\]

Sample Data:

So we will take two samples to see if the products works
  • sample using the product
  • sample without using the product

How do we know it works?

Central Limit Theorem
This says that the mean of a sample if the sample is significant will have a normal distribution with mean at the original and a similar standard deviation, specifically the original standard deviation over the square root of the sample size
Example:
If our population is:
\[P(x) = \frac{1}{ {\sigma \sqrt {2\pi } } }e^{ { { - \left( {x - \mu } \right)^2 } \mathord{\left/ {\vphantom { { - \left( {x - \mu } \right)^2 } {2\sigma ^2 } } } \right.} {2\sigma ^2 } } }\] with mean: \[\mu\] and standard deviation: \[\sigma \neq 0\]
our sample will be:
mean \[\bar{x_{n}} = \mu \] and standard deviation \[\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}\] with n the size of the sample Then if we take a random sample z: \[z = \frac{\bar{x_{n}}-\mu}{\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}}\]

To understand the Probability we need to know some definitions:

sample space
This tell us al the possible results, we use an S={...} to write this, for example if we if we toss a dice, of sample space will be: \[S_{d} = \left\{ 1,2,3,4,5,6 \right\}\]
simple event
This is a sub-group of the sample space given by some rules, for example if we are playing parchis and we need a numer from 5 onwards then our simple event will be: \[A_{d} = \left\{ 5,6 \right\}\]
The probability
Are the chances of an event happening, for example for the last sample space if we have the same probability for all the dice faces we can use the Laplace's rule Say that the probability of an event is the possible cases over the total then our probability of A will be: \[P(A)=\frac{ \sum_{ i=0 }^A Ai }{ \sum_{ i=0 }^S Si }\] \[P(A_{d}) = \frac{ 2 }{ 6 }\]
compound event
We could have events compose from two or more simple events example: with simple event A and B:
  • Event A or Event B \[A \cup B\]
  • Event A and Event B \[A \cap B\]
  • Event A given Event B \[A \mid B\]
probability distributions
Tell us the probability of events given a sample space this could be:
  • Continuous like 1,2,3,4,5,6 (like an histogram)
  • Discreet like 0-1 being 0 the the area under the curve

Array operations:
scalar
\[a .\begin{bmatrix}a & a1 & a2 & ... & an \\ b & b1 & b2 & ... & bn \\ ... & ...& ...& ...& ...\\ m & m1 & m2 & ... & mn\end{bmatrix}\]
Transpose
\[\begin{bmatrix} a & a1 & a2 & ... & an \\ b & b1 & b2 & ... & bn \\ ... & ...& ...& ...& ...\\ m & m1 & m2 & ... & mn \end{bmatrix}^{T} = \begin{bmatrix} a & b & c & ... & m \\ a1 & b1 & c1 & ... & m1 \\ ... & ...& ...& ...& ...\\ an & bn & cn & ... & mn \end{bmatrix}\]
Matrix Multiply
\[\begin{bmatrix}a1,1 & a1,2 & ... & a1,n \\ a2,1 & a2,2 & ... & a2,n \\ ... & ...& ...& ...\\ am,1 & am,2 & ... & am,n\end{bmatrix}\cdot \begin{bmatrix}b1,1 & b1,2 & ... & b1,m \\ b2,1 & b2,2 & ... & b2,m \\ ... & ...& ...& ...\\ bn,1 & bn,2 & ... & bn,m\end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}\sum a1,i \cdot bi,1 & ... & \sum a1,i \cdot bi,m \\ ... & ... & ... \\ \sum am,i \cdot bi,1 & ... & \sum am,i \cdot bi,\end{bmatrix}\]
Identity
\[ I = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & ... & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & ... & 0 \\ ... & ...& ...& ...\\ 0 & 0 & ... & 1 \end{bmatrix}\]
Inverse
If we have the matrix X: \[inverse = x^{-1}\] where: \[X \cdot X^{-1} = I\] Used to solve matrix ecuation's systems

%Gr√°fica de la Se√±al de la informaci√≥n
%Para graficarla debemos definir un tiempo contra el cual graficarla

    end

%Modulacion M-QAM
y = qam_modulator(modulation_Order,x)%Retorna arreglo con magnitud y fase

%filtro coseno alzado
%filtro tx
rolloff = 0;      %Factor de rolloff 
span = modulation_Order;      %N√∫mero de s√≠mbolos
sps = 1;		%N√∫mero de muestras por s√≠mbolo
rrcFilter = rcosdesign(rolloff, span, sps);  	%Creaci√≥n del filtro

txFilt = zeros(1, length(y)); %Se crea un vector que almacene la se√±al filtrada sin los datos adicionales aleatorios
l1 = length(txFilt)+modulation_Order/2;		%L√≠mite del ciclo for
for i = 1 + modulation_Order/2:l1        %Ciclo for para eliminar valores aleatorios adicionales
    v1 = txFiltext(i);		%Variable adicional que toma los valores de la se√±al
    aux = i - modulation_Order/2;		%Variable auxiliar para almacenar los datos en txFilt
    txFilt(1,aux)=v1;		%Almacena los valores de la se√±al sin los datos adicionales
end
